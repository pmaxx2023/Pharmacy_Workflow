EDI transaction reference

Transaction Name Direction Purpose
850 Purchase Order Pharmacy → McKesson The order itself. Every downstream transaction references this PO number.
855 PO Acknowledgment McKesson → Pharmacy Line-level accept / modify / reject. IA = accepted. IB = changed. IR = rejected.
856 Advance Ship Notice McKesson → Pharmacy Carton-level detail including lot numbers and expiry dates. DSCSA required.
810 Invoice McKesson → Pharmacy What you owe. Price vs. contract rate delta here triggers the 844 chargeback.
820 Payment Remittance Pharmacy → McKesson Net payment with adjustments applied. Closes the financial loop on the 810.
844 Chargeback Claim McKesson → Manufacturer Filed by McKesson on pharmacy's behalf. Claims the WAC-vs-contract price delta.
849 Chargeback Response Manufacturer → McKesson Accept or deny. Denial triggers dispute. Filing window misses are common.
867 Resale Report McKesson → Manufacturer Actual dispensed quantities. Validates 844 claims. Feeds manufacturer market share data.

Why McKesson's intermediary role matters for chargebacks

A pharmacy does not file chargeback claims. McKesson aggregates claims across its entire pharmacy network and submits batched 844 transactions to manufacturers. This means:

SCALE ADVANTAGE
Manufacturers receive one batched file per distributor, not millions of individual pharmacy claims.
DEPENDENCY RISK
One McKesson submission error or missed window affects every pharmacy in that batch — not just one.
867 AS EVIDENCE
The 867 resale data McKesson sends is what manufacturers use to validate the 844 quantities. Mismatch = denial.
