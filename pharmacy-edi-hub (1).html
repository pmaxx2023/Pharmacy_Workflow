Why McKesson's intermediary position matters for chargebacks

A pharmacy does not file chargeback claims directly. McKesson aggregates claims across its network and submits batched 844s to manufacturers. This creates both scale advantage and dependency risk.

SCALE ADVANTAGE

Manufacturers receive one batched file per distributor, not millions of individual pharmacy claims.

DEPENDENCY RISK

One McKesson submission error or missed window affects every pharmacy in that batch — not just one.

867 AS EVIDENCE

The 867 resale data is what manufacturers use to validate 844 quantities. Mismatch = denial.
